# JobIQ Product Brief

## Problem
Job seekers experience job search fatigue due to information overload, weak context in keyword search, and unclear trust in AI recommendations. JobIQ reduces manual scanning while keeping the user in control.

## Target users
- Andi, 22, fresh graduate. Wants fast results and over-trusts AI.
- Siti, 30, senior backend engineer. Wants highly specific roles and is skeptical of AI.

## Goals
- Cut the time to find relevant jobs from hours to minutes.
- Make the reasoning behind each recommendation visible.
- Prevent blind trust by exposing gaps and risk indicators.
- Preserve privacy for CV and preference data.

## Context of use
- Desktop browser is primary, used in personal spaces.
- Peak usage in the morning and evening.
- Users may be stressed or anxious, so the UI must be supportive.

## Value proposition
- AI scouts and summarizes, humans decide.
- Clear match reasoning with confidence bands.
- Saved jobs, comparison, and application tracker.
- Interview practice with real time feedback.

## Non goals
- No automatic job applications.
- No replacement for interviews or negotiations.
- No forced social login or public sharing.

## Success signals
- Users review 10-20 jobs instead of hundreds.
- Users can explain why a job is recommended.
- Low rate of clicks on low confidence or risky jobs.
- Positive feedback on clarity and control.

## Prototype note
This build uses mocked AI outputs via API routes and fixtures to simulate the workflow.
